##Day 20 of my Data Analytics Build Journey 

After a long break filled with unexpected hurdles, I’m excited to share that I’ve finally completed my Christmas Gift Sales Dashboard. 

Once the visuals were laid out and the slicers were properly connected, the entire narrative came together seamlessly.

Here are the key insights the dashboard revealed:

✨ Seasonal Revenue Peak
€28.53M Total Revenue was generated across markets. Seasonal peak shows highest in December (€14.90M), with  November (€7.52M) and January (€6.11M). This confirms the month of December as the peak period for gift purchases during the festive season.

✨ Top Product Categories
Toys dominated the product category with (€4.51M), followed by Clothing (€3.24M), Accessories (€2.82M), Books (€2.68M), and Building Sets (€2.23M)

✨ Customer Segmentation
Children segment leads the sales with €11.1M showing strong demand for kids-focused products

✨ Top-Performing Countries
Sweden leads country revenue with 13.4% (€3.82M), Netherlands with 13.2 (€3.80M), Germany with 13.1% (€3.75M), and United Kingdom with 13.0% (€3.66M)

✨ Top-Performing Cities
Top 5 cities contributed 20.4% of revenue, highlighting geographical concentration risk. Malmo (4.7%), Gothenburg (4.4%), Stockholm (4.3%), The Hague (3.5%), and Utrecht (3.5%).

✨ Low-Performing Markets
Austria (6.6%),
Poland (6.7%),
France (7.9%), and
Italy (8.0%)
lagged behind but shows potentials for growth

✨ Recommendation
Encourage targeted promotional campaigns in  underperforming countries during the festive season to boost sales and reduce dependency on top-revenue countries.

I am excited this project is completed. Looking forward to what the next phase of the challenge brings

Tool used: Microsoft Excel | Power Pivot | Data Visualization will evolve
